Week 9: Surface Preparation – Sanding, Filling & Finishing Your Carry-all

Introduction

With your Carry-all assembled and square, it's time to move into surface preparation — the stage that separates an average job from a professional-looking result. Whether you plan to paint, stain, or oil your project, the finish is only as good as the surface underneath.

This week is about the three-step formula for a great finish:

  • Sand smooth
  • Fill gaps or defects
  • Apply an appropriate finish

Sanding

Start with medium grit (e.g., 120) to level surfaces, then move to fine grit (e.g., 180 or 240) for a smooth finish.

  • Always sand with the grain, not across it
  • Use a block to avoid rounding edges
  • Don't oversand — it can ruin sharp edges or expose glue lines

Filling Gaps & Small Defects

Use wood filler that matches your timber:

  • Apply with a scraper or finger
  • Let it dry fully before sanding smooth

Choosing a Finish

Common options:

  • Clear polyurethane — hard-wearing, water-resistant
  • Stain + clear coat — adds colour and seals
  • Oil finish (e.g., Danish or linseed) — enhances grain
  • Paint — adds colour, hides flaws

Test on scrap first — finishes can change the appearance more than expected.
